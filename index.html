What are Websites?

Read time: 2 minutes

A website is like a digital home on the internet, where people, businesses, and organizations can share their ideas, products, or services with the world. We use websites for many different reasons—whether it’s to find information, shop online, stay connected with others, or even just for entertainment. In today’s world, having a website is important because it helps people reach a global audience, connect with customers, and make information easily accessible to anyone, anywhere.

"A website is not just a digital presence; it’s an opportunity to build a lasting connection with the world." - Lewis,2024

Websites are a key part of our everyday lives, making it easy to find information, connect with others, and explore new things. Whether it's a small personal blog or a large online store, websites help people share their ideas, sell products, and reach out to others across the globe. A good website feels welcoming and easy to use, encouraging visitors to stay, explore, and interact. In our connected world, having a website is like having a door that’s always open, ready to share what you have with anyone, anywhere.

Knife Crime Website Campaign

Read time: 2 minutes

For my second-year final major project, I built a campaign website dedicated to preventing youth involvement in violent knife crime. The website is designed to educate and engage the public through a variety of content. It features interviews with diverse voices, including a police officer who shares insights into law enforcement's role, a Town Hall member who discusses community efforts, a young person who offers a personal perspective, and an expert who provides a broader understanding of the issue. Additionally, the site includes a podcast where I share my own thoughts and experiences related to knife crime, aiming to offer a personal and reflective viewpoint on the matter.

“Together, we can turn the tide on knife crime by empowering each other with knowledge, compassion, and action.” - Lewis,2024

To further enhance community interaction, the campaign also includes an Instagram page. This social media component allows us to reach a wider audience, encourage dialogue, and share updates and resources. By combining these elements, the website not only provides valuable information but also fosters a collaborative environment where individuals can come together to address and prevent knife crime. The ultimate goal is to inspire proactive solutions and create a supportive network that contributes to the safety and well-being of young people.

Finance Foundation Campaign

Read time: 2 minutes

The concept behind creating a finance website specifically for secondary school and university students aged 15 to 25 is to bridge a significant gap in financial literacy. Many young people today struggle with managing their finances due to a lack of proper education on the subject. Research shows that a staggering 82% of students wish they had more financial education in school (Russell, 2023). This website was developed to address this need by introducing a Personal Finance syllabus into schools and providing valuable resources for students as they transition into adulthood.

"Empowering young minds with financial knowledge today builds a foundation for their success tomorrow." - Lewis,2024

The website is designed not only to educate students on essential financial topics but also to offer practical tips and solutions for managing personal finances. From budgeting and saving to understanding credit and investments, the site aims to equip students with the knowledge they need to make informed financial decisions. By integrating educational content with actionable advice, the website seeks to empower young people to take control of their financial future and foster a generation of financially savvy individuals.
